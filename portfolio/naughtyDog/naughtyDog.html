Here's a timeline of the company's event and milestones:

  • 1984: Andy Gavin and Jason Rubin created JAM (Jason and Andy's Magic) Software
  • 1986: JAM Software released its first game ever Ski Crazed for the Apple II computer
  • 1987: Published a text adventure game named Dream Zone
  • 1989: JAM Software changes its name to Naughty Dog
  • 1989: Released Keef The Thief for the Apple II computer, Amiga, and MS-DOS
  • 1991: Released an RPG game named Ring of Power for the Sega Genesis
  • 1994: Released Way of the Warrior, its first fighting genre
  • 1996-1998: Kickstarted the 3-D platformer series Crash Bandicoot (Crash Bandicoot, Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back, and Crash Bandicoot 3: Warped) which at that time was one of the most popular pop culture creations on the globe
  • 1999: Continued the Crash series with kart racing video game Crash Team Racing
  • 2001-2004: Created another platformer series Jak and Daxter (Jak and Daxter: The Precursor Legacy, Jak II, and Jak 3) that sold over 12 million copies worldwide and went on to win 7 Guiness World Records
  • 2005: Released vehicle combat game Jak X: Combat Racing for the Playstation 2
  • 2007-2016: Made a third-person shooter platform series Uncharted (Drake's Fortune, 2: Among Thieves, 3: Drake's End, 4: A Thief's End) that follows protagonist Nathan Drake, portrayed by motion-capture and voice actor Nolan North
  • 2017: Continued the Uncharted Series with Uncharted: The Lost Legacy, the first game not to feature protagonist Nathan Drake
